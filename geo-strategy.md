# GEO Strategy for AI Search Visibility
## Generative Engine Optimization (GEO) / Large Language Model Optimization (LLMO)
**Added May 2026**

---

## Overview

This field has its own name now: **GEO (Generative Engine Optimization)** or **LLMO (Large Language Model Optimization)**. It's meaningfully different from traditional SEO.

---

## The Core Shift

Unlike traditional Google search where you're competing for spots 1–10 on a results page, with LLMs you're either mentioned in the AI's response or you're invisible. There's no page 2. When ChatGPT recommends 3 businesses, those are the only 3 that exist in that user's mind.

Instead of a fixed ranking, visibility in AI search is about **frequency** — how often your brand appears across many different responses to many different prompts. Think of it as a **mention rate**, not a ranking.

---

## The Practical Playbook

### 1. Make sure AI can actually crawl your site
Check your `robots.txt` file — many sites block AI crawlers without realizing it. Cloudflare recently changed its default configuration to block AI bots, so if you use Cloudflare, your AI bot traffic may have been shut off automatically. Also consider creating an `llms.txt` file to help AI systems understand your site structure.

### 2. Build E-E-A-T authority (Experience, Expertise, Authority, Trust)
LLMs look for reliable content — well-cited, comprehensive content written by people or brands who clearly know their stuff. Back up your claims with relevant, recent stats and link to reputable sources.

### 3. Structure your content for AI
Use schema markup, clear headings, and concise formatting to help AI understand and cite your content. Specifically:
- Frame subheadings as real questions ("How do I do X?" rather than just "X")
- Add FAQ sections
- Use comparison tables and numbered lists — formats AI can easily extract

### 4. Write for natural language questions, not keywords
LLMs don't just scan for keywords like old-school Google algorithms. They read and understand content the way humans do, looking for semantic signals that demonstrate you truly understand your topic.

### 5. Get third-party mentions and citations
Both LLMs and search engines are better at recognizing brands that demonstrate expertise across a subject, not just a single page. High-authority backlinks increase your chances of being trusted and surfaced in AI answers. Reviews on platforms like Google, G2, and Trustpilot also matter — they provide trust signals LLMs pick up on.

### 6. Build topic clusters, not one-off articles
Cover your niche deeply across many pages. One great article won't establish you as an authority — an interconnected web of expert content on related topics will.

### 7. Social presence amplifies everything
LinkedIn, Twitter/X, and Reddit show particularly strong influence on LLM visibility for B2B brands.

---

## Don't Abandon Traditional SEO

Brands with the strongest share of voice in LLM responses are typically those that invested in SEO first. Strong technical health, structured data, and authority signals remain the bedrock for AI visibility. **GEO builds on top of SEO — it doesn't replace it.**

---

## Tracking Your AI Visibility

**Tools worth looking at:**
- **LLMrefs** — tracks your brand mention frequency across ChatGPT, Claude, Perplexity, Gemini, and more
- **Semrush AI Tracking** and **Ahrefs Brand Radar** — emerging AI visibility features
- **Profound** — monitors brand mentions and tone across major LLMs

You can also set up custom dimensions in GA4 to identify traffic originating from LLMs, and monitor branded homepage traffic in Google Search Console, since many users discover brands through LLM responses and then search directly in Google to validate.

---

## The Bottom Line

Make your content so authoritative, well-structured, and genuinely useful that AI has no reason not to cite you when someone asks a relevant question.
