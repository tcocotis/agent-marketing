User wants to add a GEO (Generative Engine Optimization) service to the myNHD engagement as a pilot — get paid to do it, prove it works, then potentially offer it to other clients.

**The opportunity:** When real estate agents ask AI tools (ChatGPT, Claude, Perplexity) questions like "what do I need for a California NHD disclosure?" — myNHD should appear in the answer. Right now they don't.

**myNHD GEO audit findings (May 2026):**
- Site is a transaction portal, not a content hub — almost no educational content
- Homepage is mostly an order form with minimal copy
- FAQ page has only 6 transactional questions (payment, delivery, insurance) — nothing educational
- No blog, no county-specific pages, no hazard zone explainers
- Social channels exist (Facebook, Twitter/X, Instagram, LinkedIn, YouTube) but unknown activity level
- No visible Google/G2/Trustpilot reviews
- 20+ years domain age — strong authority foundation, but unused from a content standpoint
- Integrated with Dotloop and ZipLogix — some third-party presence

**GEO gap: LARGE.** LLMs have nothing from myNHD to cite. Competitors (PropertyID, SnapNHD, First American/FANHD, CresNHD) may have more educational content.

**The fix:** Create a library of authoritative pages answering questions real estate agents ask AI tools:
- "What is a California NHD report?"
- "Is an NHD report required for commercial properties?"
- "What are the six hazard zones in a California disclosure?"
- "What's the difference between a fire hazard zone and a wildland fire area?"
- "Can the seller or agent be liable if they skip the NHD report?"
- "How long does it take to get an NHD report?"

**Three work streams for the engagement:**
1. Content strategy + production (educational pages, FAQ expansion, topic clusters)
2. Technical setup (robots.txt audit, llms.txt file, schema markup)
3. Social/review amplification (LinkedIn, third-party review platforms)

**myNHD differentiators worth amplifying in content:**
- Safe Harbor Provision — liability transfers to myNHD
- $10M E&O insurance covering all parties
- "If you don't close, you don't pay" policy
- 20+ years as industry leader
- All California counties covered
- FortressFire® wildfire report (branded product)

**Wikipedia assessment (May 2026):** Not viable right now. myNHD has no independent journalistic coverage — only directory/partner listings (CAR zipForm, ORHP, Crunchbase, Alignable). Zero press coverage, no award mentions, no executive quotes in industry publications. AB 1280 (2023 NHD law change) was a missed PR moment. No competitors have Wikipedia entries either. Path to Wikipedia runs through the GEO content strategy — build authoritative content, get quoted in industry press, generate third-party citations first. Wikipedia is a downstream benefit, not a starting point. Revisit if engagement expands.

**Status:** User has not yet scoped or priced this — next step is drafting a scope and pricing for the GEO add-on when ready.

**Why:** User wants to pilot GEO with a real client before offering it broadly. myNHD is the right test case — specific niche, clear audience, open competitive window.

**How to apply:** When user is ready to scope the GEO add-on, start with the three work streams above. Content production is the highest-leverage item.
